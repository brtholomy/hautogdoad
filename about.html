In this little patch of grass I’m supposed to leave a neat pile of well-digested fictions. I’m supposed display my willingness to cooperate in the proper exchange: while your job is to buttress another pathetic obsequious narcissism, to contribute to what will likely amount to a fugitive cultural legitimacy, and for authorizing another pantomimed interrogation of the human condition as though a mutual conspiratorial will to ignorance has not been tried before, I’m supposed to relieve you of your doubts, apologize for the status quo, contribute to a communal sense of moral immunity, and begin painting over the last shitty fresco of mundus perfectissimus…

I’m supposed to list credentials. But in our modernity, everything worthwhile is outside. The further away from the center it is, the more its potential value: yet there is such a surplus of windblown garbage out here in the corona of civilization, I cannot blame anyone for mistaking my passion for just another disposable fragment of collective neurosis - or merely another angry white male.

What do I want to say?

  • That you should read my book: if you've made it this far don't hesitate.
  • That you should listen to my music: if you want to know who I am, it's all there.
  • That I won’t waste your time, because I don’t waste mine.

There are more of us than there seems to be. The first task is to find each other - but overcoming our trusty mistrust and faithful misanthropy, cannot and should not be simple. Do not trust me, test me - and I will do the same.
