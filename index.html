The Moral Disease

Taking COVID-19 as a starting point, we ask: what is mass hysteria and why does it seem so closely related to moral phenomena? Can we exploit this grotesque spectacle of moral panic in order to learn to see and think amorally? Can we learn to distinguish between moral posturing and ethical character? If moral posturing is ubiquitous in our age, what can this tell us about ourselves? What does it reveal about our collective health? If we are as radically unhealthy as this might suggest, how did we become so? Is there a discernible history to the moral disease?

Drawing on Foucault, Nietzsche, Arendt, Freud, and many more, this is a study of moral phenomena from the Axial Age philosophical revolutions, to the religious fervor of late antiquity, to the totalitarian movements of the 20th century, to the latest illuminating tales of the “immorality of morality” in our own impotent, absurd, and yet ominous age. Once again, the spirit of skepticism and healthy thinking is tested against the histrionic fictions of the protofascist: we seem bound to repeat our mistakes, but that should not compel the courageous and rational to preemptive silence.

Increscunt Animi

Fall 2021

My voice grew confident. All concerns for innovating in the purely technical aspects of music seem trivial: what matters is that my words get across, that my voice is strong and precise in its emotional coloration, that the imagination soars, the mind reels, the heart swells.

I have discovered that "rap" - which is chant, the urform of verse and chorus, the power of an inspired human voice - is what we need. We are seeking the medicine once handed out by elders around the fire: the old songs, the incarnate power of oral transmission, the deep knowing. Ceremony is lacking, and yet still alongside us every day waiting to be rediscovered: even if it's in the guise of the pop song, we must work our way back to it, without seeking to skip ahead to a premature solemnity nor seeking to imitate a "spiritual" demeanor we have not earned...


Power and Oblivion

Coming in 2022

about

As tempted as I am to show off an acerbic cynicism regarding all projects of self-revelation, while simultaneously calling attention to a nauseating ritual of mutual narcissistic consolation - anyone reading this might already be more of an ally than that treatment assumes. Maybe it’s time for all of us artists who have hidden ourselves away and put our hopes to sleep with the almost comforting dead weight of disappointment, to be a little more honest and brave.

I’m a psychologist, author, and musician. I currently live off grid in a desert wilderness with my small tribe of animals. Most of what I am and have been is clearly legible in my work, for those who know how to read and listen. And yet what I feel is important to say here, is not what I am to myself, but what I want my work to be for a few others.

Teaching is my final ambition: I feel more than ever, that there is a small but slowly emerging cadre of young people so nauseated by the bovine comedy of this “first” world, that a tenuous window of human renaissance is hovering in the margins - where once I saw only more reasons to invest in solitude, I now see opportunities for many conversations long overdue.
