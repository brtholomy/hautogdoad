Background

  • Engineering: I'm a former software engineer at Google. My goal has been to approach spirituality with the precision and robust standards more commonly found in this field.

  • Meditation: 20+ years of practice. I've studied within the Zen, Tibetan, yogic, and Daoist disciplines: from each I learned something essential I'm prepared to pass on.

  • Psychoanalysis: I have postgraduate-level training within New York psychoanalytic circles.

Method

We discard the absurd fantasies and priestly agendas which have plagued meditative practice: I teach meditation and self-development as a form of remedial neuronal training. We're interested in the frontiers of human potential: my method seeks to exploit nonlinear emergence via conditioning of the many unconscious subsystems, which produces a far greater yield of intelligent behaviors at the conscious niveaux than the usual topdown approach. But unconscious entanglements must be attacked from several angles simultaneously to yield results: the unconscious symbolic domain is where most spiritual development stalls - thus analytic interpretation constitutes the other leading feature of my method.

When this method works, you should become more intelligent and yet receptive, energetic and yet restful, imaginative and yet factual, joyful and yet serious.

Though I cannot promise anything but a challenging incentive in the great mystery of being alive.
