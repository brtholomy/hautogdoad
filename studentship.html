Study with Bartholomy

Ambling through yet another obscure yet strangely overcrowded Parisian backalley, I once saw a sign that read, in English, "Massage Reeducation". Sometimes I wonder if my talk of "studentship" does not come off similarly ominous and perverse. Am I the intellectual equivalent of a lonely karate teacher seeking to impress you with his roundhouse kick? Or am I more like the balding depressed therapist who invites you into his stuffy cat-stained world of dust and indigestion? Or perhaps I'm already half-mad with power, strolling in a white robe through my desert compound, promising whole star systems in the afterlife to the devoted few.

There is risk in seeking out a real teacher. Transference is inevitable, idealization is possible, dissapointment is likely. But so is surprise, joy, and a sudden freefall through many previously forbidding stages of growth. I challenge my students, but I also advocate for them in ways they have not yet learned to do.

In studying with me, you are benefitting from many years of wandering, gathering, and sifting: behind me stand a long line of therapists skillful and clumsy, patients neurotic and schizophrenic, gurus false and genuine, curanderos, professors, monks, dangerous wannabes and earnest adepts.

What do I teach?

  • How to see: which means disentagling salvagable character from the habit of lying to yourself.

  • How to think: which means unlearning formerly adaptive acculturation to willful stupidity.

  • How to act: which means assimilating and magnifying your existing courage and independence.

Tools you may acquire along the way:

  • Psychoanalytic interpretation. Although I avoid using Lacanian vocabulary for good reasons, I should mention that my own analytic lineage is only few degrees removed from Lacan himself: the NYC and Parisian psychoanalytic oral tradition are therefore part of what I have to pass on.
  • Qigong technique and sitting meditation - 坐禪 in the old sense. My lineage here stems from the Liuhebafa schools of Hong Kong and the southern Daoists.
  • Intellectual development. There may be recommended and required reading from Nietzsche, Freud, and selected ancient texts.

Demarcations along the path:

  • You stop wasting time on meaningless toil.
  • You discover and articulate what you want.
  • Your standard for companionship rises.
  • You find the motivation for doing what you love.

Sliding scale fees. Phone sessions weekly. Personalized retreats available to advanced students. If English is not your first language, we can work around the problem. Español, Deutsch, 普通話.
